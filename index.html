A simpler and faster everyday life

Information Architecture (IA) Exercise

by High Intent

As we continue to serve more daily essential services, Grab’s home screen vision required us to revisit our approach to how we help customers learn about Grab’s services while helping them complete their tasks faster than ever before. IA exercise kicked off last October to help us understand how our Partner Customers could serve our customers throughout the Acquire, Growth and Mature business life cycles.

We envision a future where customers will always understand what they see and where to find what they need, thereby improving the overall conversion across all services. We conducted extensive validation exercises which included card-sorting, IA testing, user research and surveys, as well as cross-TF and user workshops.

What we learned

  • Customers are confused by various places to find save money on the homepage - Subscriptions, Challenges, Feed, Inbox
  • Customer’s mental model clashes between the desire to complete a task (Book a ride, Order food) and exploring Grab’s services (Feed)
  • Activity status contains critical information, and both visibility and accessibility should be improved
  • According to Grabbers, Search, Top 3 services and QR code scanner are the most important tools on the homepage and should be most accessible by a single hand reach, or by hand gestures (Eg. Swipe right to open camera and Pay as a potential solution).

Always delivering value

Project Avengers

by Discovery

By providing faster content load and smooth scrolling experience we saw stark uplift in feed engagement. Our customers experienced faster scrolling view and concise relevant information that made content consumption much better than previous iteration.

Moving into Project Avengers, we want to focus on surfacing relevant content to users contextually.

Key considerations:

  • Contextual information of user such as location, time of day, user status (eg. User who is in transit should see content related to his destination)
  • User app lifecycle (eg. Day 1 Grab user should see different content from a 6-month old Grab user)
  • User service lifecycle (eg. Users who are mature Transport but non-Food should discover value in adopting Food as a second service)
